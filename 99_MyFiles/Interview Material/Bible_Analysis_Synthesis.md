## The Single Biggest Risk

You have roughly 10-15 minutes of actual speaking time in a 30-minute call. That's 5-7 answers max. Your natural speaking mode is 5+ minutes per answer. **If you don't compress, you will answer 2 questions and the call will end.** Andrew will walk away knowing you're smart but unable to assess whether you're any of the other things SharkNinja cares about.

## Three Things to Drill Before Monday

### 1. Headline First, Always

Your instinct as a historian is to build context, then arrive at the conclusion. Flip it.

- **Wrong:** "When I joined Belmont, the situation was complicated because the previous records were scattered across three months of fragmented documents, some in Excel, some handwritten..." (60 seconds before you get to the point)
- **Right:** "I rebuilt three months of fragmented financial records into audit-ready statements." (5 seconds. Andrew now wants to hear more. He'll ask.)

**Practice method:** Write ONLY the headline for each story. Practice saying just the headline and then stopping. The silence after is where the conversation starts.

### 2. Framework Before Content

Every answer opens with a structural sentence, then fills in.

- **Wrong:** "Well, there were a lot of things going on at the time, and I think the most important was..."
- **Right:** "I approached this from three angles: first the data quality issue, then the stakeholder alignment, then the tooling. Let me walk you through each briefly."

This is consulting DNA made invisible. You're not "doing a case interview." You're communicating like someone who works in a CEO's office.

### 3. End with a Conclusion Sentence, Then Stop

Every answer ends with "So the key takeaway is..." and then you close your mouth. No supplementing. No "oh and also..." No "another thing I should mention..."

**The silence after your conclusion is a gift to Andrew.** It lets him ask what he's actually curious about. Follow-ups turn monologues into conversations, which is exactly what "Am I likeable?" evaluates.

## Consulting Vocabulary to Use (Without Sounding Like a Consultant)

Don't name-drop frameworks. Just think in them.

| Instead of... | Say... |
|--------------|--------|
| "I thought about it" | "I framed the problem as..." |
| "The main thing was" | "The key driver was..." |
| "I did three things" | "I structured my approach around three areas..." |
| "I found out" | "The analysis showed..." |
| "I decided" | "I recommended X, based on..." |
| "It went well" | "The impact was [number]..." |
| "I guessed" | "My hypothesis was... and I validated it by..." |
| "It was hard to choose" | "The trade-off was between X and Y..." |
| "Before I did anything" | "Before building anything, I scoped which variables drive 80% of the outcome..." |

### Hypothesis-Driven Framing (Most Transferable Skill)

When asked "How would you approach X?", always open with:
> "My initial hypothesis would be [X]. To validate that, I'd look at [Y and Z]. If the data shows otherwise, I'd pivot to..."

This signals you THINK before you compute. Exactly what the CEO office needs.

## "What Would You Do?" Answer Pattern

Use the case entry framework invisibly:

1. **Restate and scope:** "So the core question is how to [reframe their question in business terms]."
2. **Structure:** "I'd approach this as: [2-3 MECE buckets]."
3. **Go concrete fast:** Pick ONE SharkNinja-relevant example and walk through your approach.
4. **End with action:** "My deliverable would be X, on Y timeline, pressure-tested with Z stakeholder."

## Virtual Interview Execution Plan

### Setup
- Earbuds/headphones (not speakers)
- Neutral background, light source in front of face
- Join Teams 10 minutes early, test audio/video
- All notifications OFF, phone silent and face-down
- **NO documents open on screen.** Stick 3 keywords on a Post-it next to your camera

### Pacing (30 minutes)
| Segment | Time | What to Do |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Small talk | 0:00-2:00 | Smile, warm, real. Andrew has engineer vibe - be genuine, not formal. |
| Core Q&A | 2:00-23:00 | 5-6 questions. Each answer: framework sentence → key points → conclusion. 90 seconds max. Then: "Would you like me to go deeper on any part?" |
| Your questions | 23:00-28:00 | Ask 2 prepared questions. Not Googleable. Ask Andrew's personal perspective. |
| Close | 28:00-30:00 | Thank him. Express genuine excitement. |

### Energy Management
- You're nocturnal. Interview is at 1:00 PM. Make sure you're awake and alert.
- Stand up and move for 2 minutes before the call
- Have water ready
- **Do not check email or news before the interview** - avoid anything that could undermine confidence

### The Silence Rule
- 2-3 seconds of silence to organize thoughts is professional
- Say "Let me think about that for a moment" - then deliver structured answer
- Silence beats filler words ("um", "so basically", "you know")
- Silence after your conclusion beats adding "oh and one more thing"

## Pillar Balance Checklist

After the call, mentally verify you demonstrated all four:

- [ ] **People:** Did I show I'm warm, collaborative, good across cultures?
- [ ] **Business:** Did I frame at least one answer in business terms (revenue, cost, margin, ROI)?
- [ ] **Engineering:** Did I prove I build things and understand implementation cost?
- [ ] **AI Deployment:** Did I reference production AI experience (Horsys)?

If any pillar was missed, you left value on the table for the next round.
