## Four Pillars (Must demonstrate across ALL answers)

Every answer, regardless of which question it addresses, should touch at least one of these. The goal is that by the end of any interview round, the interviewer has seen evidence of all four.

| Pillar | What it proves | What it sounds like |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| **People** | I work well with diverse teams; I anticipate and meet people's needs | "I spoke with each stakeholder individually to understand what they actually needed..." |
| **Business** | I have strategic acumen and a real methodology for analyzing problems | "The core question was whether the margin erosion was structural or cyclical, so I..." |
| **Engineering** | I have the technical mindset; I know what it costs to build; I can collaborate with engineers | "I built the pipeline myself using Claude Code and Python, so when I scope a project I know..." |
| **AI Deployment** | I can deploy AI for real applications; I understand the full stack from concept to production | "The system runs in production daily, handling 60+ multilingual contacts with RAG and agentic workflows..." |

## How to Use This Framework

### When a question comes in:
1. **Identify the category** (A-H) in 2 seconds
2. **Pick which pillar(s)** to emphasize based on what you've shown so far in this interview (balance across 4 pillars)
3. **Pull the right story** and deliver in H-SOAR-L, 90 seconds

### H-SOAR-L Reminder:
- **H** = Headline (one sentence: "I'd like to tell you about when I...")
- **S** = Situation (1-2 sentences max)
- **O** = Obstacle (brief - what made it hard)
- **A** = Action (this is 60% of your time - what YOU did, structured: "I took a three-step approach...")
- **R** = Result (quantify if possible)
- **L** = Learning (one sentence connecting to why this matters for THIS role)

### Consulting Vocabulary to Weave In Naturally:
- "I framed the problem as..." (not "I thought about it")
- "The key driver was..." (not "the main thing was")
- "I structured my approach around three areas..." (not "I did three things")
- "The analysis showed..." (not "I found out")
- "I recommended... based on..." (not "I decided")
- "The impact was [quantified]..." (not "it went well")
- "I hypothesized that... and validated by..." (not "I guessed")
- "The trade-off was between X and Y..." (not "it was hard to choose")

### Pillar Balance Check:
After each interview, mentally verify:
- [ ] Did I show I'm good with people?
- [ ] Did I demonstrate business thinking?
- [ ] Did I prove I can build / understand engineering?
- [ ] Did I show real AI deployment experience?

If any pillar is missing, steer toward it in your next answer or in your closing questions.
