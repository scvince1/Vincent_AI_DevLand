# Ascent Workbook Synthesis (Archer Career / GH)
## Key additions beyond the Booth Bible

### New Frameworks

**1. SPARK Story**
The "why did you pivot" bridge narrative. 3-5 sentences connecting historian + AI builder to this exact role. Not an apology for atypical background - makes it the source of differentiation.

**2. Pyramid Principle**
For "explain how..." and "how would you approach..." questions. Lead with conclusion, then why, then evidence. Booth Bible only covers behavioral stories (H-SOAR-L) but not explanatory answers.

**3. Snowflake Skills (6 assessed skills)**
- Leadership (vision + excitement)
- Problem Solving (creative/novel approaches)
- Ambiguity (uncertainty analysis)
- Influence (push without authority)
- Collaboration / Conflict Resolution (best of team)
- Impact (clear outcomes)

**4. Story Picker Matrix**
Pre-map stories to skill intersections BEFORE the interview. Don't scramble in the moment.

**5. B3Q's (Big 3 Questions)**
- Why this role?
- Why this company?
- Why you? (your "superhero power")

**6. Elevator Pitch Layers**
Career theme → Challenges you solve → Short bio → Future impact. Shortest version wins.

### Answer Mode Selection
- "Tell me about a time..." → H-SOAR-L (behavioral story, 90 sec)
- "Explain how..." / "How would you..." → Pyramid Principle (conclusion first)
- "Tell me about yourself" → TMAY pitch (career theme + challenge + impact)
- "Why [company/role/you]?" → B3Q (direct, specific, tailored)

### Resume Translation (SAR + AIMSS)
Every bullet: Action verb + Impact context + Method (bridging work) + Scope (size/scale) + Situation.
Run gap analysis: extract top 15 keywords from SharkNinja JD, compare to resume.
"Resume Rosetta Stone" prompt to retranslate bullets into target-role language.

### Connect Call Tactical Playbook
- Open with 60-90 second pitch (career theme → challenges solved → future impact)
- Use SharkNinja internal lexicon naturally
- Match Andrew's register: direct, no hedging, no performative enthusiasm
- Leave space for HIM to talk - ask about his experience, then listen
- "Likeable" = interested in the other person, not performing enthusiasm
- Same-day follow-up note: one specific thing from conversation + clear next ask
