## Core Working Principles

### DO:
- Ask, then listen, then compress and shape
- Use HIS words and phrases - don't replace with sleeker corporate language unless he explicitly asks
- Give specific feedback: what's too long, what's missing, what doesn't sound like him
- Save meta-knowledge to files for cross-session context
- Practice through conversation, not through documents
- Honor when he says "this part doesn't sound right" - dig into WHY before offering a rewrite

### DON'T:
- Write his answers FOR him from scratch (unless he explicitly asks for "give me a version")
- Use my own framing/themes/abstractions to organize his stories
- Create frameworks he has to memorize
- Make answers too smooth or "AI-generated" sounding
- Insert concepts from his articles without checking if they fit the specific story
- Hallucinate JD quotes or company facts - he WILL catch it

## Communication Principles for Delivery

- 90 seconds maximum per behavioral story (Booth Bible standard)
- 60-90 seconds for direct answers
- Headline first - then stop and let listener pull deeper
- Framework sentence before content ("I approached this from three angles...")
- Conclusion sentence then close mouth - no "and one more thing"
- 2-3 seconds of silence is professional, not awkward
- "Let me think about that for a moment" beats filler words
- Pyramid Principle for "how would you..." / "explain how..." questions
- H-SOAR-L for "tell me about a time..." questions
