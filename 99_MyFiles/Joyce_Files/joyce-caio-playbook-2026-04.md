## 0. Context

Joyce is standing up a 6-person AI team at SharkNinja, reporting to the CEO, effectively becoming CAIO. Charter: transform current business processes; reimagine production and business lines with AI. Greenfield. Delivery format: "AI Genius Bar" as trusted thought partner for business-unit leaders. Year-1 target: build cross-org trust + a visible chain of small wins.

Team composition: 2 full-time, 2 co-op, 2 intern. All slots still open; no presets.

Joyce's own stated top concern: people management + integrating the team into the business + building trust with other leaders. Secondary ask: run the team cohesively while embedding members into departments, with resource reallocation flexibility, without being hollowed out when she is pulled elsewhere, and without ghosting embedded staff.

The spine of this playbook is Vincent's own published framework in `04_how_to_start.md` and `the_alignment_tax.md`. Joyce's situation maps almost exactly onto the COO Office deployment scenario in those documents. Main delta: 12-month visible-win pressure, tighter than the 18-month arc in the originals.

## 1. Strategic Frame

### 1.1 Name the thing correctly

Joyce's verbal framing is "AI Genius Bar." The actual build is a **centralized cognitive infrastructure team**. Genius Bar is the front-end service format; the back-end is Three-Ring KB (Corporate / Initiative / Personal) + capture pipeline + Champion Network propagation.

This distinction determines team DNA:

- If "Bar" = help desk, hiring pulls technical-support types; year-1 KPI becomes "how many leaders did we serve."
- If "cognitive infrastructure builders," every engagement deposits into the KB architecture; year-1 KPI becomes "how much did the organization's cognitive processing speed improve."

**Use the second framing with the CEO.** It directly maps to the strategic argument in `the_alignment_tax.md`: the team exists to close the gap between organizational action speed and cognitive processing speed. This positions Joyce as strategic infrastructure owner, not IT ticket queue operator.

### 1.2 Phase logic (from 04_how_to_start.md)

| Phase | Timing | Primary output |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Phase 0: Capture | Months 1-3 | Pipeline + 3 anchor engagements + 1 visible quick win |
| Phase 1: Champion | Months 3-9 | Organic "how do I get this" requests from non-anchor leaders |
| Phase 2: Synthesize | Months 9-18 | Org-level cognitive integration, Corporate KB begins paying off |

**Do not skip Phase 0 to attempt Phase 2.** Every enterprise AI deployment that fails does so by trying to demo org-level value before the capture pipeline exists. The CEO will be tempted to ask for Phase 2 deliverables by month 2. Holding the line is Joyce's job.

### 1.3 Three anti-patterns to pre-empt

1. **AI Training Day.** Any proposal to run an all-exec AI workshop to "announce the team" must be rejected. Champion Networks form via deep 1:1 personal experience, not presentations. See `04_how_to_start.md:141-150`.
2. **Help-desk drift.** "Can you also just fix our Confluence search?" requests will arrive. Every no is Charter defense.
3. **Governance capture.** Chief Legal / HR / IT will try to co-opt the team as AI review board. Invite them as co-authors of governance; do not let them annex the team.

## 2. Team Composition

Total: 6 headcount, greenfield. 2 FT + 2 co-op + 2 intern.

### 2.1 FT #1: Head of Engagement (Joyce's operating partner)

**Years:** 6-10  
**Do NOT hire:** pure AI technical backgrounds  
**Ideal background combinations:**

- Former COO Office or Chief of Staff with cross-functional rollout experience

- Has worked daily with non-technical executives
- Has translated technical solutions for board-level audiences
- Has led cross-departmental rollouts

**Disqualifiers:**
- Resume lists model names (Llama, Claude, GPT-4) as credentials
- Entire career as individual contributor on technical track
- Has never run a stakeholder meeting

**Day 1 (first 30 days):**
- (a) 1:1 with each of Joyce's 3 anchor leaders, building personal rapport

Over 6 months, FT1 gradually takes over 70% of engagement management, freeing Joyce from every PoC.

**Profile note:** see §6.3. FT1 must be an orthogonal lieutenant, not a successor candidate.

**Years:** 6+ years applied AI (shipped product, not research)  
**Education:** CS MS/PhD + applied background, or PhD with tech-lead experience  
**Capability stack:** traditional ML + LLM/RAG + data engineering; comfortable with data-constrained problems (SharkNinja's production and supply-chain data will be messy)

**Must-have signals:**
- Can articulate why their last project failed
- Public GitHub or technical writing
- Has mentored junior engineers

**Disqualifiers:**
- Kaggle-competition profile only

**Day 1 (first 60 days):**

**Background:** 2+ applied AI projects with demo artifacts; writes production-grade code; strong prompt engineering

**Day 1 role:** rapid prototyper. Every engagement: a clickable prototype within 2 weeks, not slides.

### 2.4 Co-op #2: AI Analyst / Data Scientist

**Programs:** above + MSBA programs (NYU Stern MSBA, CMU Tepper, Duke MQM, UT McCombs MSBA)  
**Background:** strong data exploration + business question formulation; can unpack "what is the leader actually asking"

**Level:** undergrad junior/senior, or MBA first-year summer intern  
**Non-negotiable:** exceptional writing  
**Background:** has written research briefs or done consulting cases

**Day 1 role:** landscape scans ("who in the industry is doing this"); business-leader-readable 2-pagers; maintaining "AI @ SharkNinja" internal digest; pre-reading 1-pagers for Bar visitors.

### 2.6 Intern #2: Comms / Story

**Level:** undergrad  
**Background:** journalism / marketing / communications + technical curiosity; has run content ops for a student org

**Day 1 role:** every PoC result becomes a case study; internal newsletter; daily Bar comms.

**This slot is the team's political load-bearing element.** A quick win that is not packaged into a story leaders can see is, politically, equivalent to the win not happening.

## 3. Hiring Process

### 3.1 Language hygiene (Joyce's eyes only)

**Risk note:** any verbal or written expression that filters candidates by ethnicity, religion, or national origin is EEOC-actionable and can end a CAIO tenure. This framing should not appear in email, Slack, interview panel discussions, or conversations with HR or FT1.

What Joyce actually wants to filter out is not demographic, it is **behavioral pattern**: over-advocating / self-promoting candidates who crowd out listening-first team dynamics; high-network-density hires who form in-groups and structurally marginalize her as a new-to-AI woman reporting directly to the CEO.

The solution is not demographic filter. It is **hiring process redesign**.

### 3.2 Six behavioral screens (FT1's interviewer training)

| # | Screen | What it catches |
|---|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Listening density** | Count candidate's questions vs statements. Bar wants questions > statements. |
| 2 | **Position update** | Mid-interview, feed new information. Do they update or defend? Bar = exploration, not debate. |
| 3 | **Failure retrospective** | "Tell me about a failed project." Listen for: blame-shifting / over-self-blame / structured "what I would change." |
| 4 | **Translator test** | Give a technical concept (e.g., embeddings). "Explain this to a SharkNinja marketing director." Gauges audience awareness. |
| 5 | **Teach-me-what-you-don't-know** | Humility test. Candidates who admit non-expertise are safer than omni-competent performers. |
| 6 | **Cross-functional panel** | Final round: 3-4 people, deliberately including non-AI SharkNinja leaders (marketing / product / ops). They vote. Filters candidates who can only converse with technical peers. |

### 3.3 Funnel redesign

Do not rely on LinkedIn + agency recruiters alone. Add:

- (a) Northeastern / CMU official co-op pipelines (pre-filtered by school)
- (b) MBA summer intern programs (translator instincts baked in)
- (c) Internal transfer posting (hidden SharkNinja employees may be right-fit; internal hires have built-in political legitimacy)

**Changing the funnel changes who shows up. Safer and more effective than filtering at the resume stage.**

## 4. Day 0 / Week 1 / Month 1 / Month 3

### 4.1 Day 0 (before hires arrive)

1. **Write the Genius Bar Charter memo** for CEO signature. Must include:
   - Mission
   - In-scope / out-of-scope
   - Explicit "things we will NOT do" (e.g., "we do not replace IT ticket workflows")
   - Failure tolerance clause: "30-40% of pilots not meeting projection is healthy; 0% failure rate = we are cherry-picking, which is Bar decay"
2. **Identify 3 Anchor Leaders** (influential + willing + real problem). None can be skeptics. Phase 0 rule: do not convert skeptics, build believers, await Planck generational turnover.
3. **Identify 3 Silent Killers** (AI-skeptical leaders who won't express publicly but will block via governance / IT / legal / HR). Pre-emptive 1:1 coffee: not to convince, but to preserve face.
4. **Lock in CEO expectation:** "First 3 months, we do not deliver insights. We deliver pipeline." Quoting `04_how_to_start.md:13-16`: "value is not better insights, it is that data starts flowing in a structured way."
5. **Reject the AI Training Day.** If HR or comms propose an all-exec announcement event, decline.

### 4.2 Week 1 (after hires arrive)

- **Day 1-2:** Product immersion. Each of the 6 takes 2-3 Shark/Ninja products home to use for a week. Not ritual onboarding; cognitive calibration. Their product intuition will shape every engagement.
- **Day 3-5:** Each team member shadows 1 business leader for a day (from the 3 anchors). Not interview; immersive observation of how the leader decides, gets interrupted, sources information. This is the Personal KB capture starting point.
- **End of Week 1 retro:** single question, "what hidden questions does the leader have that they haven't articulated?" The list becomes the pilot candidate pool for the next 3 months.

### 4.3 Month 1 deliverable (for CEO)

- 1 visible quick win (can be tiny: shared internal prompt library, meeting-summary tool, etc.) with real usage data
- 3 scoped pilots, each with a business-owner signature and a success metric
- 1 "State of AI @ SharkNinja" internal briefing deck. Frame: "what we found," not "what we plan to do"

### 4.4 Month 3 deliverable

- 2-3 completed pilots with business-owner handoff (business can run the tool independently after the team exits)
- First batch of case studies published internally
- **Gold signal:** first organic "how do I get this for my team" request from a non-anchor leader. Per `04_how_to_start.md`, this is the signal that Champion Network is growing on its own.

## 5. Operating Rhythm

The heartbeat that keeps the team coherent while embedded.

| Frequency | Event | Host | Override-able by department? |
|-----------|-------|------|------------------------------|
| Daily, 15 min | Bar standup (wherever people are) | FT1 | No |
| Tuesday afternoon, weekly | **Bar Day** (everyone returns to Bar; no department meetings) | Joyce | No |
| Weekly, 30 min | Joyce 1:1 with each team member | Joyce | No |
| Bi-weekly | Engagement review (per active engagement) | Joyce + FT1 | No |
| Monthly | Bar retreat (half day, non-project, team) | Joyce | No |
| Quarterly | Department joint review (served leaders attend) | Joyce | No |

"No override" is enforced in writing. Every engagement SOW contains: "team member's weekly Bar Day attendance is non-negotiable." If a department says "deadline is tight this week, can your AI person skip Bar Day?" the answer is always no. **Bar Day skipped once = lost forever.**

## 6. Anti-Hollowing-Out (防架空) Mechanisms

Four structural gates. Not trust-based.

### 6.1 Intake ritual as moat

Any business leader requesting Bar help must route through intake (FT1 or Joyce receives). No "I just want to grab X for a quick question" bypass. If a team member is grabbed privately 3 times, that is a live signal of Bar erosion. Escalate immediately.

### 6.2 Three authorities Joyce retains, never delegates

- **Performance narrative:** quarterly "how is X doing" story written by Joyce (FT1 can provide input)
- **Promotion / comp sign-off:** never delegated
- **Strategic visibility:** CEO, exec team, external benchmark presentations. Joyce on stage always. FT1 on stage once = the day succession narrative begins

### 6.3 FT1 profile: orthogonal, not successor

Counterintuitive but critical. FT1's next career move should be **VP Product / VP Strategy / VP Operations**, not VP AI. Hire chief-of-staff / program-runner types, not "next CAIO" types.

**Interview screen:** ask "three years from now, what do you want to be doing?" If the answer is "running an AI function," disqualify. Joyce needs a complementary partner, not a latent competitor.

### 6.4 Budget line under Joyce's name

The 6-person comp budget, engagement chargeback (if designed), tooling budget: all Joyce's cost center. Reallocation requires no sign-off from Chief Legal / HR / IT. **This must be negotiated the day the CEO names her CAIO.** Claiming it later is nearly impossible.

## 7. Anti-Ghosting Mechanisms

Protect embedded team members from isolation, department-absorption, and drift.

### 7.1 No solo embedment

Minimum 2 people per department deployment (one full-time + one half-time works). Physical isolation is the precondition for ghosting; eliminate the precondition.

### 7.2 Named return date

Every engagement SOW specifies a calendar end date. Department may request extension, but extension is **positive consent**: active request required; default is auto-return to Bar.

### 7.3 Bilateral 1:1

Joyce weekly 30 min per team member: first 10 min on work, last 20 min on welfare. "How are you actually doing" is a real question, not a checkbox. This 20 min is the earliest-warning radar for ghosting.

### 7.4 Shared artifacts obligation

Wherever someone is, they update weekly:
- Team's Initiative KB (shared)
- Their own Personal KB (private, readable by Joyce)

Three consecutive weeks without updates = red flag. Joyce intervenes directly.

## 8. Resource Shifting Protocol

To shift people between engagements without political blowback, every engagement SOW must contain these 4 clauses:

1. **Deployment window** (e.g., Apr 15 – Jun 14, 8 weeks)
2. **Re-deployable clause:** "Bar leadership reserves the right to reallocate staff with 10 business days notice for higher-priority engagements."
3. **Mini-cycle reviews:** every 3 weeks, a three-party review (Joyce + department leader + embedded member) decides continue / adjust / wind-down
4. **Exit ritual:** 1 week before engagement end, embedded member produces handoff doc + training session for the department's own people

With these 4 clauses, Joyce pulling someone is not "taking away," it is "executing the contract." Department dissatisfaction has no legitimate channel: Charter signed, SOW signed, CEO aware.

**Companion practice:** when pulling someone, Joyce proactively offers a replacement plan or handoff schedule. Relationship maintenance, distinct from contractual right.

## 9. FT1 Role Calibration (daily behavior)

FT1 is operational glue, not shadow CEO.

**Does:**
- Host standup
- Manage engagement pipeline
- Triage intake
- Coordinate team logistics
- Coach team members internally

**Does not:**
- Present strategy to CEO or exec
- Announce team policy
- Sign on behalf of Bar
- Independently decide hiring

**Opening script for FT1 meeting a new department contact:** "Joyce is working on X. I am her operating partner. The final decision on what we discuss today rests with her."

Said once each meeting, this line reduces hollow-out probability measurably. Joyce should write this into FT1's onboarding brief, not as micromanagement but as role definition.

## 10. Joyce's Personal Discipline

The commitments she is most likely to let herself skip.

1. **Tuesday Bar Day, always present.** If a meeting conflicts, push back on the meeting. Bar Day is structural contact with the team, not soft ritual.
2. **1:1s never delegated to FT1.** 25 min compressed to 15 min is fine; delegation is not. The moment 1:1s move to FT1, team loyalty begins drifting.
3. **CEO updates use "we," but presentations are Joyce's own stage.** Narrative control stays with her.
4. **Quarterly visit to each served department's leader.** Not engagement debrief; relationship maintenance. A leader who has not seen Joyce in 6 months will not think of the Bar when a new problem arises; they will think of the embedded team member who served them last time.

### 10.1 Weekly self-diagnostic heuristic

If Joyce cannot, at any given moment, answer these three questions:

- "Which engagement is X on?"
- "Who is X working with over there?"
- "What is X's current blocker?"

…then she is losing operational grip. Not answering = call back Bar Day + refill 1:1s + pull engagement review. These three practices always restore center.

## 11. Political Defense + Well-being

1. **CEO-direct is blessing and target.** Other C-level will attempt neutralization via governance / compliance / budget mechanisms. Month 1: Joyce proactively invites Chief Legal, Chief HR, Chief IT to co-author an "AI governance framework." Co-authors, not blockers.
2. **Never become the bottleneck.** Her instinct will be "I'll talk to every leader myself." This will kill her. FT1's 6-month KPI is running 70% of engagements independently.
3. **Language hygiene.** Team is never called "the AI guys" or "AI team." Call it "Genius Bar," or internally "Cognitive Infrastructure" in formal settings. Naming fixes identity; identity fixes how the team is treated.
4. **Failure management.** First PoC will fail; this is statistics. Pre-brief the CEO: "we run a portfolio; 30-40% not meeting projection is healthy; 0% fail rate means we are cherry-picking." Put this in Charter.
5. **Joyce as Bar's first customer.** In the first 30 days, pick a real problem from her own department and have FT2 run a pilot for her. She needs to experience the Bar ritual from the customer side to know what a good Bar looks like.
6. **People-management reframe.** Joyce's stated top concern is people management, which tempts her to "do it herself" to avoid managing people she is unsure of. Actual reframe: **her job is not to do AI; it is to build a team of AI-doers who trust her and each other.** Without this reframe, she burns out in year 1 and the team stalls.
7. **Hard stop.** No weekends. The Bar is service business; service business gets crushed by always-on. Week 1, Joyce announces Bar operating hours. Modeling the boundary is how the team learns to hold it.
