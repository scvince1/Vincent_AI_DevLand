## Question
What does the public record tell us about how Crossan-Matos frames consumer insights, what stack she ran at Ulta, and what tone a pitch artifact should take to land with her specifically?

## Sourced facts
1. **Ulta CMO scope (directly documented):** at Ulta Beauty she led "integrated marketing, creative, analytics, customer experience, member loyalty, public relations, consumer insights, and UB Media teams" — a portfolio that puts consumer insights as **one of seven peer functions** reporting to her, not a subordinate of marketing.
2. **Samsung background:** before Ulta she was Chief Marketing, Citizenship & Communications Officer at Samsung Electronics America, responsible for U.S. consumer AND b-to-b marketing and communications strategy. She also held SVP of Corporate Strategy, Innovation & Transformation at Samsung. She understands CE product reviews, firmware drama, and launch-cycle sentiment from the inside of a hardware company.
3. **P&G tenure:** 16 years at Procter & Gamble across skincare, cosmetics, fragrance. This is the classic CPG consumer-insights training ground — she will recognize aspect-level sentiment instantly because it's how CPG product managers have always read review data.
4. **SharkNinja role (confirmed April 29 2025):** First-ever Chief Growth Officer at SharkNinja, newly-created executive role reporting to CEO Mark Barrocas. Mandate: "redefine how SharkNinja measures success through systems and KPIs that place the consumer at the heart of every decision... amplifying and globalizing the company's consumer obsession."
5. **Speaking patterns:** she has appeared on Columbia Business School's Bizcast (brand purpose + strategy), BRAVE COMMERCE podcast (customer-centric marketing + innovation), ANA speaker roster. Her public rhetoric centers on "customer-centric" and "brand purpose" — operator vocabulary, not martech vendor vocabulary.

## Implications for the SharkNinja pitch narrative
- **Vocabulary match:** the README polish should use "consumer obsession," "customer-centric," "systems and KPIs," "place the consumer at the heart of every decision" — her own phrasings. Avoid generic "social listening platform" framing; that's vendor-speak she would have heard 500 times.
- **Peer-function framing:** present the dashboard not as a listening tool, but as the **missing input to the 7-function CGO portfolio** — specifically as an always-on feed for the consumer-insights function that currently depends on periodic manual sampling (the Maya Chen persona in requirements.md is a direct tell).
- **P&G x Samsung synthesis:** she will immediately grok aspect-level scoring because that's CPG product-management primitive. Use the word `aspect` unapologetically in the README; don't dumb it down.
- **Complementary, not competitive, to Agentforce:** she joined a SharkNinja already running Agentforce in customer service (Salesforce press release Jan 14 2025). Do NOT position as an Agentforce replacement. Position as the earned/organic substrate that complements the owned/inbound substrate Agentforce already covers. Pairs cleanly with the Agentforce-survivorship-bias framing in §9 of review-round-1.md.
- **Risk:** if the README reads like a junior-analyst pitch, it will immediately get classified as "external vendor deck #47" and ignored. Use the operator vocabulary to signal we live inside her mental model.
