## Second-Pass Summary: Historian's Voice

**Where it's strongest:** The scissors scene. The flush toilets / gas stoves / electric lighting sequence. The Pending Terms concept. The "who negotiates honestly" detail. These are moments where a specific object or action carries an argument the prose never has to state.

**Where it's weakest:** The "Capital H Human" section drifts into manifesto register — declarations about frameworks, capitalized terms without origin stories, claims about what "we" are doing. The historian's voice is most absent precisely where the essay is trying hardest to be important. The fix is not more examples — it's fewer claims. Let the architecture sections do the arguing. By the time the reader reaches the closing, they either believe you or they don't. The closing questions are strong because they ask rather than assert. The paragraphs immediately before them should trust that same instinct.

**One structural flag:** The essay's two strongest conceptual moves — "Human Sloppiness" and "we know more than we can tell" — both emerge from specific system failures. "Human Efforts" does not. It arrives as a label without an incident. Either give it one or let it go.
