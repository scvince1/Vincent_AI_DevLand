# Article Series: We Know More Than We Can Tell

Seven-article series exploring how knowledge moves — between people, organizations, and AI systems — and what that movement reveals about the humans who design and use these systems.

## Series Motif (母题)

> "We know more than we can tell." — Michael Polanyi, 1966

Every article in this series asks the same underlying question from a different angle: how does knowledge flow? Between a person and their past decisions, between an individual and their organization, between a human and an AI, between the tacit and the explicit, between who we are and who we are becoming.

Article 7 is not just one article in the series — it is the theoretical foundation for all the others.

Author framing across the series: History of Technology, MA, University of Chicago. The historian's question — how does knowledge spread, who holds it, what conditions allow it to move — applied to contemporary AI systems.

## Series Arc

Articles 1–2: Technical foundation. How to build systems that hold and retrieve knowledge.
Articles 3–6: Organizational and social layer. How knowledge moves (or fails to move) through human systems.
Article 7: Philosophical foundation. What knowledge is, where it lives, and what AI reveals about its nature.

Reading order is flexible. Article 7 can be read first as theoretical grounding, or last as synthesis.
