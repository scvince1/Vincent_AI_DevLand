# Corporate Org Map: Consumer-Signal-Relevant Roles
**Scope:** SharkNinja-scale consumer products (~$5B revenue, ~3,000 employees)
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

| Executive | Title | Owns |
|---|---|---|
| Mark Barrocas | CEO | All |

## 1. Marketing

### Career Ladder

| Title | YOE | What They Own | What They Decide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brand Marketing Co-op / Intern | 0 | Campaign assets, competitive trackers | Nothing unilaterally |
| Brand Marketing Coordinator | 0-2 | Executes campaign materials, tracks KPIs | Tactical timelines |
| Brand Manager | 2-5 | Full brand plan for one product line, budget owner | Messaging, creative briefs, agency day-to-day |
| Senior Brand Manager | 5-8 | Multi-line brand strategy, first P&L visibility | Repositioning proposals, agency RFPs |
| Marketing Director / Brand Director | 8-12 | Entire brand cluster or category; team of 3-6 | Portfolio strategy, pricing tiers, major campaign go/no-go |
| VP, Brand Marketing | 12-18 | Full brand architecture across Shark/Ninja or sub-brand | Annual brand investment allocation, agency selection |
| SVP, Global Brand Marketing | 18+ | Cross-market brand standards globally | Brand architecture decisions, M&A brand impact |
| CMO (Global or Brand-specific) | 20+ | All marketing functions | P&L co-owner with CCO |

**SharkNinja-specific title flagged:** SVP Global Brand Marketing (Laura Dyer, confirmed). Ninja and Shark treated as separate brand clusters with dedicated teams.

### Within-Function Specializations

| Specialization | What distinguishes it |
|---|---|
| Performance Marketing | Paid media (search, social, programmatic); owns ROAS, CAC; separate from brand |
| Affiliate Marketing Manager | Partner/influencer affiliate programs; owns affiliate P&L |
| Social / Influencer | Organic social + creator relationships; owns reach and engagement |
| Content Marketing | Long-form, SEO, owned media; owns content calendar and traffic |
| CRM / Lifecycle | Email/SMS flows (Salesforce Marketing Cloud at SharkNinja); owns retention KPIs, LTV |
| Paid Media Manager | Channel execution across Meta/Google/Amazon Ads; owns spend efficiency |
| Commerce Media | Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect, retail on-site ads; standalone VP at SharkNinja ($250-350K) |

**Reporting line:** Performance/Social/CRM Managers to Director Digital Marketing to VP Digital/Brand to CMO/CGO

Source: [SharkNinja Careers](https://careers.sharkninja.com/marketing)

## 2. Product (PM, Product Marketing, NPD)

### Career Ladder

| Title | YOE | What They Own | What They Decide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Associate Program Manager | 0-3 | CMF variants, packaging for legacy SKUs | Executes direction only |
| Product Manager | 3-6 | Single category roadmap element; requirements, timeline | Feature prioritization within assigned category |
| Senior Product Manager | 5-9 | Multi-SKU program; cross-functional lead | Trade-off decisions on spec vs cost vs timeline |
| Director, Product Development | 8-14 | Full category (e.g., Shark Cordless) from concept to shelf | Category roadmap, consumer satisfaction targets, P&L |
| VP, Product Development | 12-18 | Multiple categories; owns revenue + margin for portfolio | Portfolio investment prioritization, NPD greenlight |
| SVP / Chief Product Roadmap and Innovation Officer | 18+ | Global product + innovation roadmap; new category creation | Architecture of entire pipeline; $50M+ new category calls |
| CPO | 20+ | Product org globally | All product P&L; cross-functional authority |

**SharkNinja-specific title flagged:** "SVP - Chief Product Roadmap and Innovation Officer" is non-standard. Most companies separate CPO from Innovation Officer. SharkNinja combines them. Salary: $325-375K. Source: [Built In](https://builtin.com/job/senior-vice-president-product-development/6205440)

### Product Type Distinctions

| Role Type | Primary Focus | Key Output |
|---|---|---|
| Product Manager (PM) | Requirements, roadmap, timeline, cross-functional coordination | PRD, launch plan, go-to-market brief |
| Product Marketing Manager (PMM) | Positioning, messaging, launch narrative, sales enablement | Messaging framework, launch deck, battle cards |
| NPD / Product Development | Physical hardware: engineering, sourcing, CMF, safety testing | Production-ready product to BOM |
| Growth PM | Acquisition funnel optimization; DTC conversion, Amazon SEO | Conversion rate, trial/repeat metrics |

**Reporting:** PM, PMM, NPD Directors all report to VP/SVP Product to CPO to CEO.

## 3. Consumer Insights / Market Research

### Career Ladder

| Title | YOE | What They Own | What They Decide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer Insights Analyst | 0-3 | Survey builds, qual/quant data collection, report decks | Methodology within approved vendor scope |
| Market Insights Analyst | 2-4 | POS data, syndicated (Nielsen/Circana) tracking, competitive | Data pull selection and analytical framing |
| Senior Insights Manager, Market Intelligence | 5-9 | Competitive landscape monitoring, market sizing, category trends | Research agenda for a category; vendor contracts |
| Consumer Insights Manager, Product Development | 5-9 | VoC for NPD: concept testing, CLT, in-home use studies | Which insights surface to product team; testing protocols |
| Director, Consumer Insights | 10-14 | Full function: quant + qual + syndicated + UX research | Research investment allocation, report standards |
| Senior Director, Global Consumer Insights | 14-18 | Cross-market insights harmonization; insight standards globally | Methodology governance across geos |
| VP, Consumer Insights | 18+ | Insights as strategic asset; C-suite advisory role | Enterprise research roadmap; Qualtrics/panel vendor decisions |

**SharkNinja actual titles confirmed:** Consumer Insights Analyst, Consumer Insights Manager (Product Development), Senior Insights Manager (Market Intelligence). Source: [SharkNinja Careers](https://careers.sharkninja.com/)

**Reporting:** Director CI to VP CI or VP Product (dual-report common) to CGO or CPO

## 4. Customer Service / Customer Experience

### Career Ladder

| Title | YOE | What They Own | What They Decide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Customer Service Representative | 0-2 | Individual case resolution (phone/chat/email) | Resolution within policy bounds |
| CX Specialist / Senior CSR | 2-4 | Complex escalations, product safety flags, retention calls | Retention offers within policy |
| CX Team Lead | 3-5 | Queue management, real-time coaching, daily ops | Staffing coverage decisions |
| CX Manager | 5-8 | Entire contact center pod or channel (chat); CSAT ownership | Tool config, escalation protocols, QA standards |
| Director, Customer Experience | 8-12 | Full CX operation (voice + digital + social care); NPS/CSAT org targets | Headcount, budget, outsourcing decisions |
| VP, Customer Experience | 12-18 | CX strategy + technology stack + VoC integration | Platform contracts (Salesforce Agentforce at SharkNinja), workforce vendor |
| Chief Growth Officer (SharkNinja-specific) | 20+ | CX org + consumer-first growth roadmap | Enterprise CX direction |

**Note:** At SharkNinja, CX/call centers report to CGO, not a standalone CXO. This is structurally unusual at this scale. Salesforce Agentforce is confirmed CX stack. Source: [Built In Boston](https://www.builtinboston.com/articles/sharkninja-hires-chief-growth-officer-20250430)

## 5. Quality / Product Safety / Compliance

### Career Ladder

| Title | YOE | What They Own | What They Decide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quality / Compliance Assurance Analyst | 0-3 | Testing documentation, regulatory checklists, issue logging | Flag issues; no resolution authority |
| Product Compliance Specialist | 2-5 | Labeling, packaging, instruction compliance vs UL/CE/FCC | Compliance certification sign-off for specific SKUs |
| Quality Assurance Manager | 5-9 | QA function for category; audit programs; supplier QA | QA hold/release decisions; audit vendor selection |
| Director, Quality Assurance and Compliance | 8-14 | Full QA + compliance program; CPSC relationships; safety testing | Product hold escalation; regulatory submission strategy |
| VP, Quality / Product Safety | 14-20 | Enterprise quality system; cross-product safety governance | Product recall initiation (with Legal); safety standard adoption |

**Reporting:** VP Quality typically reports to COO or CPO. Safety issues escalate to Legal + CEO.

**Consumer signal relevance:** QA generates complaint and failure data that is an early-warning signal for review intelligence tools. Cross-functional Product Safety Compliance Program (PSCP) includes QA, Legal, Engineering, and Supply Chain.

## 7. Data / Analytics (Marketing + Consumer-Facing)

### Career Ladder

| Title | YOE | What They Own | What They Decide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Junior Sales Operations Analyst | 0-2 | Reporting pulls, dashboard maintenance, POS data cleanup | None; produces reports on spec |
| Insights Analyst / Analytics Analyst | 2-4 | SQL-based business/commercial analytics, Power BI dashboards | Analytical framing; metric selection within brief |
| Market Insights Analyst | 2-4 | Syndicated data (POS + panel), competitive tracking | Data source selection |
| Data Scientist, Market Insights and Analytics | 4-8 | Advanced modeling: MMM inputs, churn, segmentation, demand forecast | Model architecture; variable selection |
| Analytics Manager / Sr. Manager, Analytics | 6-10 | Team of 2-5 analysts; analytics roadmap for a function | Tech stack config (Power BI, Tableau), reporting standards |
| Analyst, Strategic Sales and Analytics (SS&A) | 3-7 | Commercial excellence: pricing, promotion, distribution analysis | Actionable recommendations to commercial/sales leadership |
| Director, Strategic Sales and Analytics | 10-15 | Full SS&A function; cross-channel analytics; Analytic Partners MMM oversight | Analytics investment, vendor contracts |
| VP / CDO | 15+ | Data strategy, governance, enterprise analytics platform | Data platform architecture, CDO-level vendor selection |

**SharkNinja-specific titles confirmed:** "Analyst, Strategic Sales and Analytics" and "Director, Strategic Sales and Analytics, US Beauty." Data Scientist in Market Insights requires 4+ YOE in consumer goods/retail/ecommerce. Analytic Partners confirmed for MMM.

**Reporting:** Analytics Managers report to Director SS&A or VP Digital to CGO or CCO (marketing analytics vs sales analytics split common).

Source: [SharkNinja Careers](https://careers.sharkninja.com/)

## 8. E-commerce / Retail / DTC

### Career Ladder

| Title | YOE | What They Own | What They Decide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ecommerce Coordinator / Operations Analyst | 0-2 | Product listings, content uploads, order flow monitoring | None; executes directives |
| Digital Commerce Specialist | 2-4 | Amazon/Walmart SEO, A+ content, catalog management | Listing optimization within brand guidelines |
| Category Analyst / Marketplace Analyst | 2-5 | Category share tracking, retailer portal analytics (Amazon Vendor Central) | Analytical recommendations to buyers |
| Senior Manager, Ecommerce Category Strategy and Insights | 5-9 | Amazon strategy + insights synthesis for category; search + content + promo | Promotional calendar, content refresh priorities |
| Ecommerce Manager / DTC Manager | 4-8 | DTC website P&L, conversion optimization, UX testing, paid traffic coordination | A/B tests on-site, promo cadence, UX changes |
| Director, Ecommerce | 8-14 | Full ecommerce P&L: DTC + marketplace + retail.com; digital shelf governance | Channel mix, marketplace fee negotiation, platform migration |
| VP, Commerce Media | 12-18 | Retail media (Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect) + DTC media | Retail media budget allocation |
| SVP / Chief Commercial Officer | 18+ | All commercial channels including ecommerce | Portfolio channel strategy |

### Channel Ownership Split

| Channel | Owner Role | Primary KPI |
|---|---|---|
| Amazon | Marketplace Manager / Sr. Manager | ACOS, Buy Box %, search rank |
| DTC (sharkninja.com) | DTC Manager / Director | CVR, AOV, LTV |
| Retail (Best Buy, Target, Walmart in-store) | Category/Retail Manager to VP Sales/CCO | Sell-through, planogram share |
| Retail Media (Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect) | VP Commerce Media | ROAS, incrementality |

**SharkNinja-specific titles flagged:**
- "VP, Commerce Media" at $250-350K is non-standard. Most companies embed retail media under VP Marketing or VP Ecommerce. SharkNinja elevated it to standalone VP, signaling retail media is a major P&L driver.
- "Senior Manager, Ecommerce Category Strategy and Insights" is non-standard; the "Strategy and Insights" suffix signals analytics embedded in ecommerce, not a separate team.

## Signal Intelligence Tool: Who Cares About Your Output

| Function | Why They Need External VoC | Decision They Make With It |
|---|---|---|
| Consumer Insights | Validates or challenges qual research | Research agenda prioritization |
| Product Dev / NPD | Failure mode detection pre/post-launch | Feature/spec trade-off decisions |
| Quality / Safety | Complaint signal triangulation | Product hold / recall trigger |
| Marketing (Brand) | Sentiment drift and positioning gaps | Campaign messaging pivots |
| Ecommerce | Review-driven content refresh | A+ content, listing copy updates |
| CX / CGO | Voice of consumer back to product teams | CX process redesign |
| Analytics / SS&A | Signals to feed into MMM + forecasting | Budget reallocation |
