## Section 3: Vertical Structure

SharkNinja uses a **mixed centralized + vertical overlay model**, not fully vertical-siloed teams.

**Centralized functions:**
- Commerce Media / Retail Media team is centralized (VP, Commerce Media oversees Retail Media + Amazon + DTC across brands)
- DTC Digital Experience is a centralized org managing the unified SharkNinja.com (previously separate SharkClean.com and NinjaKitchen.com merged into one DTC site as of 2025)
- Data Science and Analytics - Amazon is centralized with a Global Amazon Reporting Center of Excellence mentioned in the Director, DS&A role

**Vertical overlay (confirmed by title suffixes):**
- "Marketing Specialist, Amazon US Shark Beauty": Shark Beauty has its own Amazon marketing specialist
- "Demand Generation Specialist - Amazon Shark Products": separate Shark Products-specific role
- "Director, Strategic Sales and Analytics, US Beauty": Beauty vertical has a dedicated SS&A director
- LATAM Pure Players has its own Senior Director and Director reporting structure (geographic split, not brand split)
- EMEA has separate Commerce Media Director and Senior Director

**Key inference:** Amazon commercial performance (sales, P&L, Vendor Central) lives in the **Sales org under Pure Players** branding. Amazon media and retail media spending lives in the **Commerce Media / Marketing org**. DTC (sharkninja.com) is a third separate track. The IC-level "Digital Shelf Manager" title does not appear to exist at SharkNinja by that exact name.

## Section 6: Three Key Naming Conventions (Insider Fluency)

**1. SharkNinja calls marketplaces "Pure Players"** (French retail term for pure-play online retailers), not "Amazon channel" or "marketplace." Using "Pure Players" in interview signals insider fluency.

**2. The digital-shelf / analytics function at senior level is called "Commerce Media,"** not "E-Commerce." "VP, Commerce Media" oversees Retail Media + Amazon + DTC media together. "E-Commerce" titles live mainly in Sales/account-facing roles and DTC site roles; the data-and-media layer uses "Commerce Media." A signal intelligence tool pitches to the Commerce Media persona, not the E-Commerce Manager persona.

**3. Vertical branding appears in IC titles but not director-level.** "Marketing Specialist, Amazon US Shark Beauty" carries the brand name; "VP, Commerce Media" does not. IC-level target users likely have "Shark Beauty," "Shark Products," or "Ninja" in their actual title.
