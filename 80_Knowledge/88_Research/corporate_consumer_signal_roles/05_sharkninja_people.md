## Section 7: Surprises and Patterns

**1. The Wayfair pipeline.** Both Bailey Marquis-Wu and Emily Fagerstrom came from Wayfair site merchandising. Wayfair alumni carry strong product content and search optimization instincts, the closest civilian analog to digital shelf management. **Your target user persona should look like a "Wayfair site merchandising alumna at SharkNinja Senior Manager level."**

**2. Matt Dubow's Reckitt background is highly relevant.** Reckitt (Lysol/Finish channel management at Amazon) was an early mover in digital shelf scoring; he almost certainly understands content health metrics, Buy Box strategy, and A+ content at a practitioner level. Potential champion for the tool.

**3. VP Commerce Media is still actively being recruited as of February 2026.** The role was described as "new, high-visibility, enterprise-impact." The Challenge 5 tool would slot directly into this function's mandate.

**4. The finance-to-Amazon-ops track.** Elliot Mitchell and Beth Lutz are a recurring pattern: SharkNinja grew its Amazon business through operational muscle before building a content/media layer. Tool positioning should acknowledge that Amazon at SharkNinja is currently operations-heavy, content/signal-light.
