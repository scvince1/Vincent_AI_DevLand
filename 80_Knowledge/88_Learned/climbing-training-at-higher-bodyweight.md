# Climbing Training at Higher Bodyweight

## Impact of Weight Gain on Climbing
- Every kg gained requires proportionally more finger force on every hold
- At 91kg vs 80kg = ~14% more demand on finger flexors
- Three areas hit: contact strength (finger force), endurance (forearm pump accumulates faster), power-to-weight (explosive moves suffer)
- Lattice Training data: climbers with BMI >25 can still climb hard, but technique and absolute strength become critical compensators

## Strength Training That Helps Without Weight Loss
- **Heavy, low-rep pulling:** Weighted pull-ups, 3-5 reps at 80-90% 1RM, 3-5 sets. Drives myofibrillar strength with minimal hypertrophy
- **Rows and scapular work:** Heavy barbell/dumbbell rows, inverted rows, face pulls
- **Core anti-rotation and tension:** Pallof presses, front lever progressions, hanging leg raises
- **Antagonist pressing:** ~25% of training time for shoulder health

## Heavier Climber Training Differences
- Footwork is the biggest weapon — legs can handle bodyweight, arms cannot sustain it
- Drill precision foot placement 1-2 grades below limit
- Volume on wall matters more than intensity — more moderate routes with perfect technique
- Rest longer between burns
- Prioritize route reading and efficiency

## Hangboard Alternatives (TFCC-Safe)
- **No-hang block pulls (Tension Climbing "The Block"):** Wrist stays neutral, load adjustable from very light. Top recommendation for finger strength with wrist injuries
- **Pinch block training:** Targets thumb and pinch strength, wrist in safe position
- **Rubber band finger extensions:** 10-20 reps daily for antagonist balance (Hooper's Beta protocol)
- **Wrist stabilizer training:** Eric Horst protocol — pronation/supination and radial/ulnar deviation with light dumbbells

## Balancing Hypertrophy with Climbing
- Focus hypertrophy on climbing prime movers: lats, rear delts, biceps, forearms
- Avoid building non-functional mass (excessive chest, anterior delts, traps)
- Neural strength protocols (80-90% 1RM, 3-5 reps) for climbing muscles = strength without proportional size
- Periodize if possible: hypertrophy block → strength/power block, climbing performance dips during hypertrophy and rebounds during strength
- Can climb 5.12-5.13 at 91kg but requires significantly more finger strength and technique than at 70kg
