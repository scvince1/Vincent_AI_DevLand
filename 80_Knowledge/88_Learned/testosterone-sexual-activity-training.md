# Sexual Activity, Testosterone & Training Performance

## Core Finding: Sexual activity does NOT impair training performance
- Martínez-Rodríguez et al. (2025, Physiology & Behavior): RCT n=21 trained males. Post-masturbation: testosterone briefly rose, endurance +3.2%, grip strength slightly up. No performance harm.
- Diaz-Lara et al. (2024, MDPI systematic review): 7 trials, n=115. Small biomarker changes do NOT translate to measurable performance differences.
- No Nut November study (2025, J Sexual Medicine): Month-long abstinence showed no hormonal or health improvement.

## Alcohol Impact (More Significant)
- Ranganathan et al. (2023, Expert Review of Endocrinology & Metabolism): Low-moderate acute intake may briefly raise testosterone; heavy drinking clearly suppresses it.
- Parr et al. (PLOS ONE, still best evidence): Post-training alcohol + protein → MPS still drops 24-37%.
- No new RCT on moderate drinking threshold (2-4 drinks) since 2023.

## Practical Implication
- Sexual activity: optional log field, does NOT influence training recommendations
- Alcohol: important modifier, especially post-training; agent should lower next-day intensity and emphasize protein intake
