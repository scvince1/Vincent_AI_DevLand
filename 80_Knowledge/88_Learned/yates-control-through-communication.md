The vertical filing cabinet (promoted by Library Bureau from 1890s) was sold as a space-saving device. Its actual effect was transferring institutional memory from senior clerks' heads into a queryable physical system.

Key consequences:
- Companies could reassign client relationships without ritual handover for the first time
- Companies could dismiss veteran employees without losing the business knowledge they carried
- Managers could bypass middle layers and directly access frontline transaction records
- The "clerk" role lost its pathway-to-management semantics, because business knowledge no longer flowed through the act of copying letters

Yates' framing: the filing cabinet enabled "control through communication" - better tools for managing internal information flows led to better organizational coordination and products.

Relevance: the vertical filing cabinet is the direct historical ancestor of organizational cognitive infrastructure. AI knowledge systems are being asked to do something of the same order.

Also see: Craig Robertson, "The Filing Cabinet: A Vertical History of Information", University of Minnesota Press, 2021 (more recent, more detailed material history).
