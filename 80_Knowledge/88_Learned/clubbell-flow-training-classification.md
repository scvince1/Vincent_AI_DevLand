# Clubbell Flow Training: Classification and Evidence

## Research Status: Almost None Directly
- One EMG conference abstract (Ison 2020): upper trapezius and rectus femoris highest activation
- No 2023-2026 peer-reviewed journal articles on clubbells specifically
- Best analog: kettlebell research

## Classification: Metabolic Conditioning / Aerobic-Resistive Hybrid
- Estimated 5-9 METs (based on kettlebell analog: Farrar 2010, Ainsworth 2024 Compendium)
- NOT traditional resistance training, NOT active recovery
- Track by time (minutes) + weight used, not reps/sets
- Higher complexity and heavier weight (25lb) pushes toward vigorous end

## Boxing Comparison: NOT Interchangeable
- Boxing follow-along: 8-12 METs, higher cardiovascular demand
- Clubbell flow: 5-9 METs, more intermittent, higher peak neuromuscular demand
- Boxing = cardio session; clubbell = conditioning/skill work

## TFCC Compatibility
- Controlled rotational loading is CONSISTENT with TFCC rehab protocols (Kha 2023, Abouelnaga 2023)
- Progressive rotational loading = part of recovery pathway, not avoided
- At 25lb: wrist bracing recommended
- Key rule: keep arc controlled, mills safer than 360s, pain = stop
