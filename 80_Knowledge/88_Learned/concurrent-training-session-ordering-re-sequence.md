# Session Ordering for Concurrent Training: The RE Sequence Effect

## Core Molecular Mechanism: AMPK vs mTOR

- Resistance exercise activates mTORC1 signaling (via P70S6K, 4E-BP1, S6), driving muscle protein synthesis and hypertrophy.
- Endurance exercise activates AMPK, which phosphorylates Raptor and directly inhibits mTORC1 signaling.
- When endurance follows resistance (R then E), AMPK activation from the endurance bout can suppress the mTORC1 response that was initiated by the resistance bout.
- Key finding: the last exercise bout performed dictates the dominant molecular response. AMPK activity is intensity-dependent and returns to baseline relatively quickly post-exercise.

## The RE Sequence Effect in Practice

- The 2025 Frontiers semi-systematic review (42 studies) found: acute molecular responses are clearly sequence-dependent, but long-term adaptations show less consistent sequence effects.
- For strength/hypertrophy goals: performing resistance FIRST preserves the anabolic signaling window. Endurance performed after resistance interferes less with strength gains than the reverse order.
- For endurance goals: sequence matters less. Both orders produce similar endurance adaptations in most studies.
- Practical rule: prioritize the training goal. If strength is the primary goal, always lift first. If goals are balanced, default to resistance-first because the interference is asymmetric (endurance is more robust to ordering effects than strength).

## Optimal Inter-Session Spacing

- Separating resistance and endurance sessions by 6+ hours significantly reduces the acute interference effect compared to same-session concurrent training.
- The 2024 Medicine review confirmed: interference on strength was stronger when both modes were performed within the same session vs separated by 6-24 hours.
- 24-hour separation is ideal but impractical for most recreational athletes training 4-6x/week. 6-8 hours is the pragmatic minimum.
- If same-session is unavoidable: resistance first, followed by endurance, with intra-session rest of at least 15-20 minutes.

## Endurance Modality Matters

- Running produces greater interference than cycling due to higher eccentric muscle damage in the lower body.
- For concurrent athletes prioritizing lower-body strength: prefer cycling, swimming, or rowing as endurance modalities over running when possible.
- Climbing is primarily an upper-body isometric/concentric activity and produces less lower-body interference than running. However, high-volume bouldering generates significant grip and upper-body fatigue that can impair subsequent pulling exercises.

## Practical Guidelines for Strength + Climbing + Equestrian

- Default order within a day: strength session (AM/midday) then climbing (PM), separated by 6+ hours.
- Equestrian is low-interference for upper/lower strength but generates core and hip fatigue; schedule riding on separate days from heavy squat/deadlift when possible.
- On days where same-session concurrent training is necessary: compound lifts first, climbing/cardio second, never reverse.
- Volume threshold: interference increases meaningfully when endurance volume exceeds ~30 min per session at moderate-high intensity. Keep concurrent endurance bouts under this when strength is the priority session.
