# Alcohol and Muscle Protein Synthesis: Evidence Verification

## The 24-37% Claim (Parr 2014)
- From a HIGH-DOSE study: ~1.5 g/kg alcohol (≈7-8 standard drinks for 70kg person)
- Has NOT been directly replicated in 2023-2026
- Has NOT been refuted either — still the most-cited acute measurement
- Best understood as ceiling under high-dose + concurrent-exercise conditions

## Dose-Response: Moderate ≠ Heavy
- 2-3 standard drinks (~0.5 g/kg): MPS reduction exists but NOT quantified in any human exercise trial
- Steiner et al. (2015): moderate alcohol did NOT impair hypertrophy in rodents
- Shi et al. (2023): dose-response confirmed in mice — higher BAC = longer/stronger mTORC1 suppression
- Protein co-ingestion partially rescues MPS (Levitt 2023)

## The Real Harm for Social Drinkers: SLEEP
- Gardiner et al. (2025, Sleep Medicine Reviews, 27-study meta-analysis): REM sleep reduction starts at just 2 drinks (0.5 g/kg)
- REM is critical for GH release and neural recovery
- This is arguably the underappreciated pathway for training adaptation loss

## Practical Rules
- Post-training 0-4h: highest MPS interference window; avoid drinking here
- If drinking: consume 30-40g protein alongside to partially attenuate
- Next day: no need to dramatically reduce training intensity unless sleep was visibly poor
- Track: amount + timing relative to training
