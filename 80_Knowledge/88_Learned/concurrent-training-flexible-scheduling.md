# Concurrent Training for Flexible Weekly Schedules

## Interference Effect: Mostly Negligible
- Recent meta-analyses (2024-2025): concurrent training does NOT significantly reduce hypertrophy or maximal strength vs resistance training alone
- Interference mainly hits explosive power (irrelevant for hypertrophy/climbing goals)
- For climbing specifically, interference is LOCAL to pulling muscles (lats, biceps, forearms)
- Pushing muscles (chest, triceps, front delts) barely affected by climbing

## Scheduling Rules
- Climb Monday → Push Tuesday: works well, minimal overlap
- Climb Monday → Pull/Back Tuesday: NOT ideal, pulling muscles need 48-72 hours after hard climbing
- Place back/pull hypertrophy 48+ hours after hard climbing
- CNS recovery from hard climbing: 24-48 hours; connective tissue: 36-72 hours

## RPE/RIR-Based Auto-Regulation
- Program by movement pattern, not specific exercises
- Train to target RPE 7-8 (RIR 2-3) for hypertrophy sets
- Progressive overload = same RPE, accumulate reps across weeks, bump load when exceeding rep target
- Makes program fully equipment-agnostic (Zourdos et al. 2016, Helms et al. 2016)

## Moderate Cardio Impact
- Moderate-intensity cardio 2-3x/week does NOT meaningfully impair hypertrophy
- Critical variable is caloric intake, not the cardio itself
- Badminton and horse riding can go on any day without interference
- Only same-session cardio + strength (within 20 min) shows moderate interference; separate by 3+ hours
