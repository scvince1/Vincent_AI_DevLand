# Psyllium Husk (洋车前子壳)

**Date added:** 2026-04-11
**Source:** Dietary supplement, taken 10g dissolved in water with inulin

---

## What It Is

Seed husk of *Plantago ovata* (native to India/Pakistan). The active fraction is arabinoxylan, a branched hemicellulose polysaccharide with high molecular weight.

**Classification:** Soluble, viscous, non-fermentable dietary fiber. This is its defining profile and separates it mechanistically from most other fiber supplements.

---

## Mechanism

On contact with water, psyllium forms a thick, stable gel in the GI lumen. This gel:

- Increases chyme viscosity, slowing gastric emptying and nutrient absorption rate
- Binds bile acids and cholesterol in the small intestine, trapping them for fecal excretion
- As bile acid pool shrinks, the liver upregulates LDL receptors to pull circulating LDL as a cholesterol source for new bile synthesis
- Provides bulk and water retention in the large intestine, normalizing stool consistency in both directions

Unlike fermentable fibers (inulin, FOS, beta-glucan), psyllium is largely not broken down by colonic bacteria, so it does not directly produce short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) or provide significant prebiotic substrate.

---

## Evidence-Backed Benefits

| Outcome | Evidence |
|---|---|
| LDL reduction | FDA qualified health claim; meta-analysis (41 RCTs, 2,049 participants): LDL -8.6 mg/dL, Total-C -9.1 mg/dL |
| Blood glucose | Slows glucose absorption; reduces post-meal spikes 10-20%; improves HbA1c ~0.5-1.0% in T2DM/metabolic syndrome |
| Constipation | Adds bulk and hydration to stool; well-established laxative effect |
| Diarrhea | Gel absorbs excess luminal water; normalizes loose stool |
| Satiety | Slows gastric emptying, extends fullness; supported by weight-loss meta-analyses |

---

## Dosage

- Effective range: **5-15g per day**, typically split into 1-3 doses
- FDA health claim threshold: **7g/day** for cardiovascular benefit
- **10g (Vincent's current dose)** is solidly in the therapeutic range and consistent with most RCT dosing
- Titration: start at 2.5-5g daily for 1-2 weeks, then increase by 2.5-5g increments to minimize bloating/gas during adaptation

---

## Hydration Requirement (Critical)

- Minimum: **240 ml (8 oz) of water per 5g psyllium**, for 10g that means at least 480 ml, taken immediately
- Drink the mixture before it thickens fully; do not let it sit
- Esophageal obstruction and asphyxiation are documented risks when psyllium is taken dry or with insufficient fluid
- Fatal choking cases are on record (ISMP Canada bulletin); never take capsules or powder without a full glass of water
- Extra ambient hydration throughout the day (an additional 500+ ml) is advisable

---

## Timing Relative to Food, Medications, Supplements

- **Medications:** Take psyllium at least **2 hours before or after** any oral medication. The gel physically traps compounds and can reduce absorption of thyroid hormone (levothyroxine absorption reduced 20-30%), diabetes drugs, anticoagulants, and others
- **Mineral supplements (iron, calcium, zinc):** Separate by 2 hours minimum; gel can bind charged minerals and reduce their absorption
- **Food:** Taking 20-30 minutes before a meal maximizes satiety and glucose-blunting effects; post-meal use is also fine for regularity purposes
- **Best default timing:** Before the largest meal of the day, fully hydrated

## Psyllium + Inulin Interaction

These two fibers are complementary, not redundant, and do not blunt each other:

| Property | Psyllium | Inulin (4g dose) |
|---|---|---|
| Fermentability | Non-fermentable | Highly fermentable (prebiotic) |
| Primary site | Small intestine (gel, viscosity) | Large intestine (microbiome substrate) |
| Gas production | Minimal | Moderate, can cause bloating especially at higher doses |
| Microbiome effect | Broad shift (Akkermansia, Parasutterella) | Targeted Bifidobacteria/Lactobacilli growth |

Taking both together is a well-established stack. Psyllium's non-fermentable gel may buffer some of inulin's gas-generating fermentation, providing a gentler experience than inulin alone. 4g inulin is a reasonable prebiotic dose; if bloating occurs, consider splitting the inulin dose or timing it away from psyllium.

---

## Notes for High-Protein Lifter

**Protein absorption:** Psyllium does not meaningfully reduce protein absorption in healthy individuals. The gel slows the absorption rate but extends the absorption window, net protein uptake is not significantly impaired. Current evidence does not support concern for athletes on high-protein protocols.

**Practical timing:** Avoid mixing psyllium directly into a protein shake if protein absorption timing is critical (e.g., immediate post-workout window). Separate by 30-60 minutes as a conservative buffer; otherwise co-ingestion is not a documented clinical problem.

**Creatine:** No direct interaction data exists for psyllium + creatine. As a precaution, treat creatine like other supplements and separate by at least 1 hour, as the gel could theoretically slow creatine absorption rate. Absolute uptake is unlikely to be affected since creatine is absorbed throughout the small intestine.

**Sodium:** Psyllium is naturally sodium-free in pure husk/powder form. Verify that any commercial product (Metamucil flavored varieties) does not contain added sodium or sweeteners.
