# Equestrian Hip Mobility & Stability Training

## Critical Hip Muscle Groups for Riding
| Muscle Group | Function |
|---|---|
| **Iliopsoas** | Most important rider muscle. Connects spine to legs. Controls pelvic tuck, neutral spine, shock absorption |
| **Adductors** | Hold rider to saddle, absorb concussion. Must train WITH glutes, not in isolation |
| **Glute max/med** | Control hip rotation, primary extensors for posting trot, saddle balance |
| **Deep lateral rotators** (piriformis, obturators, gemelli) | Fine leg adjustments, cue precision |
| **Hamstrings & hip abductors** | Balance, leg position control |

## Exercises
**Mobility (daily / pre-ride, 10-15 min):** Hip CARs, 90/90 hip rotations, couch stretch, leg swings (sagittal + frontal)

**Strength/Stability (2-3×/week, 20-30 min):** Sumo squats, lateral lunges, clamshells, single-leg glute bridges, Copenhagen adductor holds, squatting circles

**Already complementary:** Animal flow (multi-planar hip mobility), mace/club work (rotational stability)

## Interaction with Heavy Lifting
- Complementary, not conflicting
- Full-ROM squats and deadlifts build exactly the glute/hamstring/quad strength riders need
- Always train through full ROM; partial ROM tightens hip flexors
- Add single-leg variations for asymmetry and stabilizer work
- Post-squat hip flexor stretching prevents adaptive shortening

## Weekly Volume
- Daily: 10-15 min hip CARs + 90/90 (morning or warm-up)
- 2×/week: 20-30 min dedicated hip strength (fold into existing sessions)
- Pre-ride: 10 min hip opening sequence
- Total: ~2-3 hrs/week, mostly overlapping existing training

## Old Hip Injury Notes
- Start with low-load isometrics before dynamic movements
- CARs map available ROM without load (especially valuable)
- Favor active mobility over aggressive passive stretching
- Watch for impingement (pinching in front of hip during deep flexion or internal rotation)
- At 91kg, bodyweight alone is significant load
- Regular foam rolling/lacrosse ball on piriformis and deep rotators is preventive
