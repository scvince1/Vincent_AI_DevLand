# Unconventional Equipment for Hypertrophy

## Sandbag
- No direct RCT comparing sandbag vs barbell for hypertrophy
- Haugen et al. (2023, BMC Sports Science): Free-weight vs machine meta-analysis shows no hypertrophy difference → implement type doesn't matter if effort is equated
- 45kg sandbag is sufficient load for upper body hypertrophy near failure

## Clubbell/Mace
- NO peer-reviewed hypertrophy evidence whatsoever
- Strictly a mobility/stability/conditioning/rotational tool
- At 15-25lb, below hypertrophy threshold for major muscles
- Potential TFCC therapeutic benefit at controlled arcs

## Light Dumbbells (12lb)
- Lees et al. (2025, J Physiology): Load does not determine hypertrophy if taken to failure
- Carvalho et al. (2023, BJSM): All load prescriptions comparably promote hypertrophy
- Can work for lateral raises, curls, etc. but requires high-rep sets (20-30+)

## Effective Reps Theory
- Baz-Valle et al. (2024, Sports Medicine): Near-failure reps are the primary stimulus regardless of load
- Refalo et al. (2024): 1-2 RIR produces equivalent hypertrophy to failure

## Bodyweight
- Push-ups produce equivalent pec hypertrophy to bench press (Kikuchi & Nakazato 2017, still uncontradicted)
- Pull-ups and dips near failure are legitimate hypertrophy tools
