# Sub-Failure Training with Light Loads: When It Works

## Core Finding: Load × Proximity-to-Failure Interaction Is Real
- At ≥60% 1RM: RIR 2-3 produces equivalent hypertrophy to failure (McKendry 2024, Robinson 2024)
- At 30-60% 1RM: RIR 1-2 needed, marginal zone
- At <30% 1RM: Must go to failure or very close (RIR 0-1) — stopping early fails to recruit high-threshold motor units (Burd 2024 AJP-Cell Physiology, Lasevicius 2022)

## Why Light Load + Sub-Failure Fails
- Low-load hypertrophy depends on fatigue-driven motor unit recruitment and metabolic stress
- Stopping at RIR 2-3 with <30% 1RM = insufficient fiber recruitment and metabolic stress
- Volume compensation (more sets) does NOT rescue qualitatively insufficient stimulus

## Practical Load-Effort Matrix
| Relative Load | RIR Target | Example at 91kg |
|---|---|---|
| ≥60% 1RM | 2-3 | Sandbag, push-ups, barbell compounds |
| 30-60% 1RM | 1-2 | Lighter dumbbells on compounds |
| <30% 1RM | 0-1 | 12lb dumbbells (balcony only, use only for suitable exercises like lateral raises) |

## Key Implication
When only light implements available, prefer heavier movement variant (sandbag over dumbbell) rather than pushing light implements to failure.
