# Clubbell Rotational Stability: Evidence Summary

## Shoulder/Scapular Stability: STRONG Evidence
- 2024 RCT (PMID: 38432789): Scapular stabilization exercise → improved dyskinesis, pain, strength, disability
- 2024 EMG study (PMID: 38432822): Bottom-up kettlebell grip (similar instability to clubbell) → highest rotator cuff activation
- Turkish get-up pattern matches clubbell flow mechanics

## TFCC/DRUJ: Controlled Rotation = Recovery Mechanism
- 2023 WSRP study (PMID: 37904835): TFCC injury impairs forearm rotational proprioception; sensorimotor training restores DRUJ stability
- 2023 non-operative TFCC program (PMID: 37490953): Progressive rotational loading → NPRS pain 5.3→0.5/10
- Avoidance of rotation is counterproductive for TFCC recovery

## Optimal Integration
- As warm-up (10-15 min) before pressing/pulling → primes rotator cuff without fatiguing prime movers
- Separate session when volume increases (heavier mills/360s)
- Frequency: 3x/week improvement, 2x/week maintenance

## Equipment Update
- Three pairs: 15, 20, 25 lb
- BDG = warm-up toy only
- 25lb: wrist bracing recommended
- Pain = stop, shorten arc
