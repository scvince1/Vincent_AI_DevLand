# TFCC Injury: Training Modifications

## Highest-Risk Movements
- Barbell bench/overhead press with pronated grip (ulnar deviation under load)
- Reverse-grip anything (supination under load torques TFCC)
- Heavy straight-bar curls (fixed wrist position creates shear)
- Wrist curls / reverse wrist curls (direct stress)
- Kettlebell snatches / Turkish get-ups (ballistic loading through end-range)
- Barbell front squats clean grip (extreme wrist extension)
- Push-ups on flat hands (sustained wrist extension)

## Lower Risk (Confirmed Safe)
- Deadlifts (neutral trap bar ideal, or hook grip)
- Squats (low bar or SSB eliminates wrist load)
- Pull-ups (neutral or overhand)
- Cable rows (neutral grip)

## Grip Modifications
- **Neutral grip is king** — least TFCC stress position
- **Fat grips / thick bar adapters** — distribute force, reduce ulnar-side point-loading
- **Wrist-neutral position** — avoid end-range extension/flexion under load; slight extension (10-15°) is safest
- **Axle bar for deadlifts** — fat-grip benefit + grip stabilization
- Keep braces on (always)

## Mace/Club Training & TFCC
- Light mace/club (15lb range) trains rotational forearm stability and eccentric wrist control — exactly what TFCC rehab targets
- Risk: end-range leverage when club fully extended behind head
- Rule: keep arc controlled, mills safer than 360s, two-handed safer than single-arm
- Verdict: beneficial at current weight IF range limits respected

## Climbing with TFCC
- Full crimp loads TFCC aggressively — use open-hand grip as default
- Slopers safer than crimps for TFCC
- Gastons and side-pulls create ulnar deviation — flag as warning moves
- Buddy-tape ring + pinky finger to limit ulnar deviation
- Volume over intensity: top rope > limit bouldering; avoid dynos and campus board
- Warm up forearms thoroughly (rice bucket or light gripper)

## TFCC Recovery/Resilience Exercises
- Eccentric wrist pronation/supination with 2-5lb dumbbell (most cited rehab exercise)
- Rice bucket work (multi-directional, zero impact)
- Isometric ulnar/radial deviation holds against wall
- Forearm pronation/supination with hammer (choked up)
- Squeeze ball work (grip endurance without wrist torque)
