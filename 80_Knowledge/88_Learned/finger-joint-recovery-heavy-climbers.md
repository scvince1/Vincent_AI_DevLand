# Finger Joint Recovery in Heavier Climbers

## Bodyweight and Finger Stress
- BMI >21 → statistically higher pulley/tendon injury rates (cross-sectional data)
- At 91kg, single-hand crimp loads can reach 45-55kg equivalent
- A2 pulley forces: 400-600N — within documented tissue failure range (260-380N threshold)
- Lutter et al. (2024 BJSM): overweight status + bouldering = increased primary injury risk

## Small Hands + Heavy Body Theory
- Biomechanically coherent: smaller joint cross-section + higher load = elevated stress per unit tissue
- No dedicated clinical study as of 2026, but consistent with first principles
- Ergonomics lit: smaller hands produce lower absolute grip force, force concentration per contact point increases

## Recovery Timeline
- Connective tissue (tendon/pulley) remodeling lags 48-72h behind muscular recovery
- Frank et al. (2023, Current Sports Medicine Reports): 48-72h minimum for recreational-intensity loads
- For 91kg climber: 48-72h is clinically plausible and likely conservative
- NOT pathological — normal connective tissue biology at higher absolute loading

## Recommendations
- 48-72h between climbing sessions (strong recommendation for this profile)
- Default to open-hand grip (reduces PIP flexion angle → less pulley stress)
- DRUJ stability prehab for bilateral TFCC
- Agent should alert if climbing detected within 48h of last session
