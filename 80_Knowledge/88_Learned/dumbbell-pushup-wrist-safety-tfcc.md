# Dumbbell Push-Up Wrist Safety for TFCC

## Neutral Wrist IS Safer: Supported
- Polovinets 2018 (J Hand Therapy): Neutral routes force through bone/joint structures; extended routes through ligament/soft tissue
- Explicit clinical recommendation: neutral wrist for post-injury loading

## BUT: Load Exceeds Clinical Thresholds
- Standard push-up at 91kg: ~29-31kg per hand
- Clinical TFCC single-hand WB tolerance: ~16kg (Andersson 2023)
- Gap: 13-15kg above measured tolerance
- Bracing adds ~4kg capacity (WristWidget study 2021)
- Kneeling variant: ~23-25kg/hand (closer but still above)

## Why It Works Despite the Numbers
- Progressive adaptation over time
- Bracing provides real additional support
- Pain-guided progression is the clinical standard
- Bilateral distribution (vs clinical one-hand test)

## Safety Rules for Agent
- Bracing = MANDATORY, not optional
- Pain = absolute stop signal
- Bad wrist day → auto-downgrade to kneeling
- Always remind to wear braces when recommending push-ups
- Never program push-ups without neutral wrist device
