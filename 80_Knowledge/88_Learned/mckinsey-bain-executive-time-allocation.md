## McKinsey (2013)
- Survey of 1,374 executives at GM level or above
- 39% of working time spent in internal meetings
- Only 24% working alone
- 61% say at least half their decision-making time is ineffective
- Only 52% spent time matching organizational strategic priorities

## Bain
- Senior leaders and middle managers spend 50%+ of time in meetings
- One weekly exec committee meeting consumed 300,000 person-hours annually (including cascade prep time)
- A studied insurance company's exec committee: 40% of time on "informing" members, only 20% on actual decisions

## Asana (2023)
- 58-60% of knowledge workers' time on "work about work" (coordination, info search, status chasing)
- Only 40-42% on skilled/strategic work
- Survey of 9,615 workers across US, UK, Australia, France, Germany, Japan

## Synthesis
The "30% alignment tax" claim is conservative. McKinsey shows 39% in meetings; Bain shows 40% of that is informational sync; that alone yields ~16% on pure information alignment in meetings. Add email, chat, informal context-setting, and 30% is well within evidence range.
