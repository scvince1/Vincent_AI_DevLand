# Inulin (菊粉)

**Date added:** 2026-04-11
**Source:** Dietary supplement, taken 4g dissolved in water with psyllium husk

---

## What It Is

Inulin is a soluble, fermentable dietary fiber extracted primarily from chicory root (*Cichorium intybus*), which contains the highest natural concentration (~15-20% dry weight). Classified as a fructan, it consists of fructose chains linked by β(2→1) bonds that human digestive enzymes cannot break down. This classification places it squarely in the prebiotic fiber category: it reaches the colon intact and serves as substrate for beneficial bacteria.

---

## Mechanism

In the colon, bifidobacteria and lactobacilli selectively ferment inulin, producing short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs), primarily butyrate, propionate, and acetate. Butyrate is the primary fuel source for colonocytes (colon-lining cells) and supports gut barrier integrity. The selective feeding of bifidobacteria also competitively suppresses pathogenic bacteria and reduces intestinal pH, which improves mineral solubility and uptake.

---

## Evidence-Backed Benefits

- **Microbiome:** Robustly increases *Bifidobacterium* and *Lactobacillus* populations; well-replicated across trials
- **Mineral absorption:** SCFA-driven pH drop and upregulation of calcium-binding proteins enhance Ca and Mg absorption; effects more pronounced when baseline intake is low; 8-10g/day threshold cited for bone mineral density benefits
- **Glycemic:** A 2025 study in obese subjects found chicory inulin shifted microbial pathways toward butyrate production and improved whole-body insulin sensitivity, with measurable reductions in fasting blood glucose vs. placebo
- **Satiety:** Modest effect via gut hormone modulation (GIP, GLP-1); supports weight management as secondary benefit

---

## Typical Dosage

| Range | Context |
|---|---|
| 2-5g/day | Ramp-up phase, first 1-2 weeks |
| 5-10g/day | Maintenance prebiotic dose |
| 10-30g/day | Doses used in research trials |
| >20-30g/day | Threshold where GI symptoms become common |

**4g is conservative, bottom of the effective curve.** It is a sensible starting dose, especially if new to inulin. Comfortable tolerance typically builds over 2-4 weeks.

---

## Side Effects

- **FODMAP:** Inulin is a high-FODMAP fructan. People with IBS-D or SIBO may experience significant bloating, gas, cramping, or loose stools even at low doses
- **Gas/bloating threshold:** Generally tolerable below 10g/day for most people; symptoms spike above 20-30g
- **Who should avoid:** IBS-D (diarrhea-predominant), active SIBO, ragweed/daisy allergy (cross-reactivity with chicory)
- **Adaptation:** Most gas/bloating in new users is transient, typically resolves within 2-4 weeks as microbiome shifts

---

## Interaction with Psyllium Husk

**They stack well and complement each other.** The mechanisms are orthogonal:

- Psyllium is a gel-forming, viscous fiber, it bulks stool and slows transit
- Inulin is a fermentable prebiotic, it feeds bacteria and produces SCFAs

A 2022 RCT (PMC8995815) found that co-administering psyllium with inulin dramatically reduced inulin-induced colonic gas (from 3145 to 618 mL·min) and breath hydrogen (from 7230 to 1035 ppm·hr). The mechanism: psyllium's high viscosity physically delays inulin's transit to the colon, slowing fermentation rate and reducing acute gas load.

**Practical implication for Vincent:** taking both together (as done today) is a smart combination. The psyllium acts as a gas buffer, making inulin more tolerable. No timing conflict, taking together is supported by the research.

---

## Notes for a High-Protein Lifter

- **Protein digestion:** High-protein diets increase ammonia and branched-chain fatty acid production in the colon. Inulin's butyrate production and bifidobacteria enrichment help counterbalance this, supporting a healthier colonic environment under heavy protein load
- **Gut transit:** Inulin + psyllium together support regularity, which matters when protein intake is high (risk of slower transit and constipation, especially with creatine use)
- **Mineral absorption:** Enhanced Ca and Mg absorption is directly relevant for a lifter, both are critical for muscle contraction, recovery, and bone stress during training
- **Sodium context:** Inulin has no sodium and may indirectly support blood pressure via SCFA-mediated gut-brain axis effects; no conflict with sodium-reduction goal
- **Creatine:** No direct interaction studied; improved gut transit and reduced gut inflammation from inulin use may marginally support creatine uptake consistency
