# Private Chef Operation (Macalester / Minnesota)
**Time:** Junior year at Macalester College, 2021-2023
**Location:** Minnesota (Twin Cities / Macalester area)
**Status:** Concluded when Vincent graduated / moved on

## Real Facts
Junior year at Macalester, Vincent's family went through a sudden bankruptcy. Some of the lost savings had been money Vincent himself had saved and family had taken earlier — there is a real betrayal layer to this period, not just financial shock. After tuition was paid, Vincent had approximately $6,000 left to live on.

Pivot: Vincent built a private-chef / meal-prep / catering business out of his living situation. It started as informal dinner gatherings, which became recurring meal-prep clients, which became catering for small events. Peak revenue was roughly $40,000 annually. EBITDA was between 50% and 70% depending on the dish mix — so real profit was in the $20K - $28K range, not $40K. Taxes were not properly filed during this period; at the time, this was technically tax evasion, though scale was small.

**Hidden truth #1: Client mix.** One of the "recurring clients" was actually Vincent's then-girlfriend (the ex now, post-depression, post car-accident jaw injury). She "paid" by covering Vincent's rent and core living costs while he cooked for her and cared for her during her recovery. External meal-prep volume was lower than the catering-business story implies; a real chunk of revenue was really this live-in caregiving arrangement.

**Hidden truth #2: Family money.** Despite the bankruptcy narrative and the emotional estrangement from parents around COVID, Vincent did continue to receive some family financial support during this period. He neither publicly nor internally acknowledges this; the estrangement is emotionally real, and the money came despite the distance. This is a charged area.

**Hidden truth #3: Tax status.** Not filed correctly at the time. Small scale, but a legal exposure if surfaced.

- Plans get destroyed. What matters after: how fast you read the situation, find what's already around you, and take the next logical move from where you actually are (not from where the plan said you would be).
- This is the spiritual sibling of the Yunnan "build with whatever's available" philosophy, re-expressed under financial pressure instead of material pressure.
- Resource inventory in a crisis includes social capital: existing relationships were the first paying customers.
