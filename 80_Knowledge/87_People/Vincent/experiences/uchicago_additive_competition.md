# UChicago Additive 3D-Printing Heating-Material Consulting Competition
**Time:** UChicago period (graduate)
**Location:** UChicago, Chicago, IL
**Status:** Concluded; team did not win the competition

## Real Facts
This was a **consulting competition**, not a real paid engagement. The "client" was a UChicago chemistry lab spinoff with a patented 3D-printable heating material. Real client, real outreach, real deliverables, but the format was competitive with multiple teams submitting plans.

Vincent led a 4-person team:
- Chemistry PhD
- Biomed PhD
- Civil engineering master's student
- Vincent — a historian with zero business / finance training going in

The team did not win. One teammate was effectively a slacker; they barely showed after the first meeting. The other two teammates were competent technically but mediocre in the business sense, and they mostly produced structural research and technical/compliance background rather than business-case output.

Vincent's actual leadership work:
- Crashed a PE (private equity) class at Booth to get at least a surface-level vocabulary for deal structure, market sizing, and financial modeling.
- Self-taught Excel via YouTube to build basic financial models.
- Wrote one-page briefs for each teammate framing their contribution in the language they each already spoke (chemistry brief, biomed brief, civil brief) so each could execute without having to learn consulting-speak.
- Built the final deliverable slides using HTML5 rather than PowerPoint — early adopter move for sharper visuals.

After the competition, the founder eventually raised a Series A, but via a separate path not causally tied to the team's specific plan. The team's competition output was not the direct driver of that outcome.

## Key Insights Extracted
- Cross-functional leadership = give each specialist a shape they can fill in their own native language, then stitch the pieces together. Don't force everyone into consulting-speak.
- When you lack the domain training (Vincent had no business background), identify the minimum viable vocabulary and go get it fast (Booth class crash, YouTube).
- Medium choice matters. HTML5 slides were a signal of craft, not just a tool pick.
