# Yunnan Border Volunteer Teaching & Building (Gōujiē Xiāng)
**Time:** ~5 years of engagement, roughly 4 deployments across summers and winters
**Location:** 耈街乡 (Gōujiē Xiāng), 保山市 (Baoshan), Yunnan Province, China — near the Myanmar border
**Status:** Concluded (foundational biographical period)

## Real Facts
Vincent volunteered with a nonprofit education program serving remote students in 耈街乡, Baoshan, Yunnan. The engagement spanned roughly 5 years, with approximately 4 deployments across both summers and winters. The area is close to the Myanmar border, though exact distance from any combat zone is uncertain.

On the ground, the work was physical and infrastructural: schools, farms, and water dams, built largely by hand with whatever tools and materials were available locally. Program served remote-area students who had limited access to standard schooling. Vincent was one of the deployment coordinators, not merely a participant, but someone responsible for helping organize the logistics of each trip.

The conditions were hard. Weather, terrain, tools, and local realities all shaped what could be done in any given deployment. Vincent came out of it with what he later described as a "hardy" disposition: hardened by hardy people in a hardy place.
