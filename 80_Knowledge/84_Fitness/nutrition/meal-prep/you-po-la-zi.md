# 油泼辣子 / You Po La Zi (Chinese Chili Oil)
_Created: 2026-04-12 | Source: web-fetch (nutritionvalue.org, FatSecret, EatThisMuch)_
_Type: reference condiment card_

## What It Is
Homemade Chinese condiment: hot oil poured over dried chili flakes with salt, MSG/chicken bouillon, sesame seeds. Vincent's version uses less salt and MSG than commercial products.

### Source Notes
- **Lao Gan Ma:** Label says 2 tbsp (30g) = 220 kcal, 280mg Na, 21g fat (FatSecret). Cross-confirmed: nutritionvalue.org reports 60g = 422 kcal, 550mg Na. Consistent.
- **Lee Kum Kee Chiu Chow:** 1 tsp (5g) = 35 cal, 140mg Na (FatSecret + EatThisMuch). Higher Na because it contains soy sauce and fermented soybeans -- less comparable to pure 油泼辣子.
- **S&B Crunchy Garlic:** 1 tsp (5g) = 35 cal, 90mg Na (Kroger/Instacart). Garlic-forward, not a direct 油泼辣子 analogue.
- **Best commercial analogue for homemade 油泼辣子: Lao Gan Ma** (chili flakes + oil + salt/MSG, no soy sauce).

## Capsaicin Note (Bonus)

Dried chili flakes contain capsaicin with documented metabolic and anti-inflammatory benefits. Quantification for homemade is not feasible, but regular low-dose consumption is generally considered beneficial. Not a tracking target.
