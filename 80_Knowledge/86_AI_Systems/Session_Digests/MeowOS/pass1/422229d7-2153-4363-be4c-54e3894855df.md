## Reading Between the Lines
- The article is a **job search artifact**. Vincent explicitly connects it to "很难服众" as a humanist in AI. The NYT/LinkedIn placement, the CDO audience, the "humanist built this" framing - this is a positioning piece designed to make him legible to hiring decision-makers who don't understand why a historian belongs in AI.
- The escalation from "GitHub technical post" to "NYT feature for executives" reveals Vincent's real ambition: he doesn't want to be seen as a practitioner sharing tips, he wants to be seen as a thinker with a framework. The audience shift from developers to CDOs is the tell.
- The "Capital H Human" motif is not just rhetoric - it is Vincent's core brand thesis. He is building a professional identity around the claim that humanist judgment is the missing variable in AI deployment. Every system he builds is evidence for this argument.
- Vincent's three-tier enterprise KB model (personal + initiative + company RAG) with initiative-end embedding is a genuinely novel architectural proposal that he chose to defer to a separate article - suggesting he has more publication ambitions beyond this piece.
- The repeated emphasis on "不要假定任何我没有明确跟你表达的 details" suggests past frustration with AI assistants making assumptions.
